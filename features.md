## Cross-Cutting Feature Themes

### Table-Stakes Features
- Demand forecasting (historical data-driven, minimum)
- Reorder point and safety stock calculation
- Replenishment recommendations
- Multi-location inventory management
- Integration with e-commerce platforms (Shopify minimum) or ERP
- Inventory analytics and KPI dashboards
- Supplier and purchase order management
- Seasonal and trend adjustments
- Lead time integration
- Alert system for low stock/reorder events

### Differentiating Features
- ML-driven demand forecasting with external signal integration
- Multi-echelon network optimisation
- Service level and fill rate optimisation
- Real-time demand sensing and continuous forecasting
- What-if scenario planning and simulation
- Probabilistic forecasting capturing demand uncertainty
- Supplier collaboration and VMI support
- Advanced analytics (demand pattern segmentation, ABC analysis)
- Integrated production planning (for manufacturers)

### Underserved Areas / Opportunities
- Continuous probabilistic models replacing static reorder points
- External signal enrichment (social media, news, competitor pricing)
- Natural-language explanations for replenishment recommendations
- Automated multi-echelon rebalancing via reinforcement learning
- Supply disruption scenario generation and contingency planning
- Demand sensing from unstructured supplier and customer signals
- Cross-company forecasting consensus (buyer + supplier + retailer)
- Predictive supplier risk scoring
- Dynamic safety stock policies by segment

### AI-Augmentation Candidates
- ML models adapting continuously to demand shifts
- LLM enrichment of demand signals from external sources
- Natural-language explanations of replenishment logic
- RL-based multi-echelon rebalancing
- Generative AI scenario simulation for supply disruptions
- ML-based supplier risk prediction

## Legal & IP Summary

SCOR framework, GS1 standards, VMI practices, and ABC/XYZ classification are industry-standard methodologies with no IP encumbrances. Multi-echelon inventory optimisation (MEIO) is an established analytical framework described in academic literature with no known patents. ISO 28000 is a published standard. No software patents identified as encumbering inventory optimisation platforms.
